06 : Interactive Scatter

Description

This interactive scatter plot visualizes total fatal aircraft injuries over time (starting from 1980) and allows filtering by the number of engines. Each point represents an incident, with the y-axis displaying the number of fatal injuries and the x-axis marking the date of occurrence. The color of each point encodes the extent of aircraft damage, with red indicating "Destroyed," yellow for "Substantial," orange for "Minor," and gray for "Unknown." Unfiltered, the data demonstrates that the majority of incidents involve significant aircraft damage, particularly "Destroyed" outcomes, and cluster closer to lower fatality counts. High-fatality incidents are relatively rare but visible as outliers.

When filtered by the number of engines:

  • 1 Engine: The most incidents occur here, highlighting that single-engine aircraft are more prone to fatal accidents, possibly due to their prevalence in general aviation. Most incidents cluster around 0–20 fatalities.
  • 2 Engine: Two-engine aircraft show fewer total incidents than single-engine planes but include some of the higher-fatality incidents. This suggests a different operational risk profile, potentially tied to larger or more complex operations.
  • 3 Engine and 4 Engines: These aircraft show much sparser data points, with incidents being infrequent but often involving higher fatalities when they do occur. These findings align with the lower overall use of multi-engine planes compared to single- and twin-engine types.

The marks in this visualization are the scatter points, while the channels are color (representing the level of damage) and position (date and fatality count). The tasks for this visualization include identifying temporal trends, understanding the distribution of fatal injuries across aircraft types, and analyzing damage levels over time. However, this graph primarily highlights individual incidents and their details. For a broader analysis, it may be more useful to examine summed fatalities per month for different engine types, as seen in the next visualization, to capture overall trends and seasonality across time. This approach could provide more actionable insights for safety interventions and operational planning.
