06 : Fatalities Over Time


Click the buttons to view total aircraft fatalities faceted by the amount of damage the plane has received. The slider can be dragged to set the minimum number of fatalities needed for incidents to be shown on the graphs. Highlighting a specific section of the graph will zoom in on it. Hover over data points for more information about the specific incident.

This scatter plot visualizes total fatal aircraft injuries over time, starting from 1980, with filtering options by the severity of aircraft damage. The marks in this visualization are scatter points, each representing a single incident. The channels include:

  • Position: The x-axis represents the date of occurrence, and the y-axis shows the number of fatalities.
  • Color: Indicates the level of aircraft damage, with red for "Destroyed," orange for "Substantial," yellow for "Minor," and gray for "Unknown."

Faceting by damage level provides insights into the nature and severity of incidents:

  • Destroyed: The most common category, with incidents clustering around 0–20 fatalities, though high-fatality events appear as notable outliers. This reflects the catastrophic nature of incidents resulting in total aircraft destruction.
  • Substantial: Incidents show a wide spread of fatality counts, though generally lower than in the "Destroyed" category, indicating serious but often survivable events.
  • Minor: Rarely observed, reflecting less severe outcomes with minimal fatalities.
  • Unknown: Sparse data in this category, but occasional high-fatality events suggest gaps in reporting or ambiguous classifications.

Filtering options allow users to focus on specific fatality ranges, addressing the high volume of low-fatality incidents that can obscure trends. The zoom feature provides additional flexibility, enabling closer examination of specific time periods or clusters of incidents. This visualization supports tasks such as identifying temporal trends, examining the distribution of fatal injuries across damage levels, and exploring changes in incident severity over time. While it highlights individual incidents, aggregated views of fatalities by month or year may offer complementary insights into overall safety trends and inform targeted interventions.
